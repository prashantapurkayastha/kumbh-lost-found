# Milan — 2-Minute Video Pitch Script

**Format:** Talking head OR screen record with voiceover. Keep cuts tight.  
**Target runtime:** 1:55 – 2:05

---

## [0:00 – 0:20] THE HOOK

> *Open on crowd footage or a photo of Kumbh Mela — dense, saffron, overwhelming.*

"Kumbh Mela. 660 million pilgrims. The largest peaceful gathering on Earth.

And on a single peak day — up to 100,000 people separated from their families.

A child lost in that crowd. A grandmother. A pilgrim from Tamil Nadu who doesn't speak a word of Marathi.

The current system? A loudspeaker. A paper register. No search. No photo. No way to close the loop."

---

## [0:20 – 0:40] THE GAP

> *Cut to a split screen: official ICCC dashboard on one side, a hand-written help desk register on the other.*

"The government's AI stack is world-class — crowd prediction, traffic flow, CCTV monitoring.

But it stops at the person level. Nobody built last-mile human reconnection.

That's the gap. That's Milan."

---

## [0:40 – 1:20] THE DEMO

> *Screen record — move fast, cut on each action.*

"A family describes their missing daughter — in Marathi, by voice, without typing a single word."

> *Show VoiceFirstScreen. Press and hold. Transcript appears.*

"Claude hears them. In their language.

It searches a live registry of every found person across 200 help desks — updated every 3 seconds."

> *Show the tool call: `search_found_persons`. Then a match card appears.*

"A match. 78% confidence. Blue kurta. Eight years old. Ramkund Center, 20 minutes ago.

Claude books Reunion Point 3. Sends an SMS. Alerts the desk volunteer.

All in one step. No human coordinator. No phone tag."

> *Cut to Volunteer Panel. Photo upload. AI auto-fills the form in 3 seconds.*

"On the other side — a volunteer uploads a photo of the found child. Claude Vision reads it, fills the intake form in 3 seconds.

A handover PIN is issued. Nobody is released without it."

> *Cut to Help Desk — PIN entry screen.*

"The family arrives. Quotes their PIN. Reunion confirmed. PII deletion countdown starts — DPDP compliant."

---

## [1:20 – 1:45] WHY IT WINS

> *Back to talking head or clean slide.*

"This isn't a chatbot. Claude takes real actions — it files records, sends SMS, books a physical desk, flags suspicious claimants to police.

It works in 8 Indian languages with no translation layer. It works offline. It costs 6 cents per incident.

At Kumbh scale — 100,000 incidents — that's $6,400 a day to reunite 100,000 families."

---

## [1:45 – 2:00] THE CLOSE

> *Hold on the app home screen. Then fade to the Milan logo / title card.*

"The help desks already exist. The ICCC already exists. Milan is the multilingual AI layer that connects them — and closes the loop.

It could be live at Kumbh 2027 in three months.

Milan. Every second counts."
